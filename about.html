01 — my story

From rushed to slow,
from consumed to curated.

A few years ago, I lived in a beautiful-ish flat that never quite felt like mine. It was full of things I'd collected quickly — furniture I'd bought cheap without thinking, objects that had been given to me, art prints I'd chosen in a hurry. I was busy, I was rushing, and my home reflected that.

Then, during a long, slow autumn, something shifted. I started replacing things carefully, one at a time. I started spending Sunday mornings rearranging objects rather than scrolling. I found that the act of making my space more beautiful made me feel more peaceful — and that this was worth writing about.

Nest of Aesthetics was born from that shift. It's a record of how I live, what I love, and what I'm learning about the small architecture of a beautiful home.

02 — how I shop & style

Slowly, carefully,
and always honestly.

I don't recommend things lightly. Every single product and object I share on this space is something I genuinely love, use, and cherish in my own home. Before anything makes its way onto this site, it has to be a true part of my daily life, bringing both quiet utility and aesthetic joy to my personal space.

When you see a link on Nest of Aesthetics, it's because I have lived with that piece, used it repeatedly, and truly adore it. I only partner with brands whose products have earned a permanent place in my home and my heart, and I will always share my honest, firsthand experience of loving and using them.

Beauty, for me, is about surrounding ourselves with things that bring us genuine happiness. This space is a collection of the products I actually love using, shared to help you curate a home that feels warm, intentional, and beautifully lived-in.
